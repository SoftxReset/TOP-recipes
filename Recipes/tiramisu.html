Tiramisu

Description

This easy to make classic Italian dessert is layered with coffee-soaked and rum flavored ladyfingers, creamy mascarpone custard and whipped cream. No baking necessary!

Origins of Tiramisu

Translating to "pick-me-up," tiramisu is a layered Italian dessert having dated back to the late 1960s in the region of Treviso, Italy. Ado Campeol, a famous Italian restaurateur, is credited as the inventor of tiramisu, and is considered to be the "father of tiramisu."

There's Rum?

Traditionally, tiramisu is to be made with Marasala wine instead of rum. It is commonly made with coffee and rum, as it does in this recipe.

However, whether you prefer the additional flavoring or not, rum can be subsituted with a rum flavoring, or entirely left out. If the latter, the coffee used would have to be extra strong in order to compensate.
