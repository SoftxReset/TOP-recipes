Tiramisu

Description

This easy to make classic Italian dessert is layered with coffee-soaked and rum flavored ladyfingers, creamy mascarpone custard and whipped cream. No baking necessary!

Origins of Tiramisu

Translating to "pick-me-up," tiramisu is a layered Italian dessert having dated back to the late 1960s in the region of Treviso, Italy. Ado Campeol, a famous Italian restaurateur, is credited as the inventor of tiramisu, and is considered to be the "father of tiramisu."

There's Rum?

Traditionally, tiramisu is to be made with Marasala wine instead of rum. It is commonly made with coffee and rum, as it does in this recipe.

However, whether you prefer the additional flavoring or not, rum can be subsituted with a rum flavoring, or entirely left out. If the latter, the coffee used would have to be extra strong in order to compensate.

Ingredients

Here's what you'll need to make tiramisu:

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup of white sugar
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 pound mascarpone cheese
  • Â¼ cup strong brewed coffee
  • 2 tablespoons rum
  • 2 (3 ounce) packages ladyfinger cookies
  • 1 tablespoon unsweetened cocoa powder

Steps to Make Tiramisu

  1. The Filling: Add in and cook the egg yolks, sugar and milk until the mixture is slightly thickened. Allow it to cool, then chill in the fridge for about an hour. When the mixture has fully chilled, mix in the mascarpone cheese.

  2. The Whipped Cream: Add the vanilla extract to the heavy cream and beat it until stiff peaks form.

  3. The Ladyfingers: Mix the coffee and the rum into a small bowl. Split the ladyfingers in half, lengthwise. Then pour the coffee-rum mixture over the ladyfingers.

  4. The Tiramisu: Assemble the tiramisu by lining the bottom of a baking dish with the soaked ladyfingers. Then spread the mascarpone mixture across the top of the ladyfingers. Layer the whipped cream over the mascarpone. Repeat until unable and dust the top with cocoa powder.
