Potato Latkes

Descrption

In Ashkenazi Jewish cuisine, a latke is a potato pancake. Traditionally served during Hanukkah, the latke can be made with grated, shredded or mashed potatoes, and can be served as a side or as the main dish.

Suggested Additions to Latkes

Common toppings to latkes are applesauce and sour cream, making it a flexible snack. As a breakfast item, it's recommended to serve latkes with more proteins, such as smoked salmon and/or poached egg.

Ingredients

  • 3 medium russet potatoes, peeled
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon grated onion
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ cup peanut oil for frying

Steps in making Latkes

  1. Slice the peeled potatoes and run them through a food process with the grater attachment. Then place the shredded potatoes in a cheesecloth and wring throroughly.
  2. Take 2 cups of the shredded and dried potatoes and place into a bowl. Add egg, flour, onion and salt. Stir until combined.
  3. Heat 1/4 inch of the peanut oil in a large skillet over medium to high heat. Place large spoonfulls of the potato mixture into the hot oil and press down. This should make thin potato patties. Cook until golden brown, about 4-5 minutes. Flip the patties and repeat on the other side.
  4. Remove the latkes from the oil and allow them to drain while cooking any remaining latke "batter." Serve hot.
